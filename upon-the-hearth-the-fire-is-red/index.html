Upon the Hearth the Fire is Red

VERSE • Mar 31, 2021

Hey Nerds, welcome to the first post of 'verse' series. Three is Company, the third chapter of Book One of The Fellowship of the Ring, contains the poem Upon the Hearth the Fire is Red by J.R.R Tolkien.

Origin-

When the hobbits are walking through the Shire's Southfarthing to Buckland, they start humming a walking tune. Bilbo Baggins wrote the lyrics, which were set to a melody that was "as old as the hills." During their walks through the Water-streets, valley's he had taught Frodo the poem. The sound of hooves could be heard after the hobbits had finished singing the tune.

The Poem-

Upon the hearth the fire is red,

Beneath the roof there is bed;

But not yet weary are our feet,

Still round the corner we may meet

A sudden tree or standing stone

That none have seen but we alone

    Tree and flower and leaf and grass,

    Let them pass! Let them pass!

    Hill and water under sky,

    Pass them by! Pass them by!

   

Still round the corner there may wait

A new road or a secret gate,

And though we pass them by today,

Tomorrow we may come this way

And take the hidden paths that run

Towards the Moon or to the Sun.

    Apple, thorn, and nut and sloe,

    Let them go! Let them go!

    Sand and stone and pool and dell,

    Fare you well! Fare you well!

   

Home is behind, the world ahead,

And there are many paths to tread

Through shadows to the edge of night,

Until the stars are all alight.

Then world behind and home ahead,

We'll wander back to home and bed.

    Mist and twilight, cloud and shade,

    Away shall fade! Away shall fade!

    Fire and lamp, and meat and bread,

    And then to bed! And then to bed!
